# Speech, Arms, and Algorithms

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## The US as a Statistical Outlier

-   The US has more privately owned firearms per capita than any other country
-   Gun death rates in the US are four to ten times higher than comparable wealthy democracies
-   Gap not explained by differences in violence rates generally
-   Specific to guns
    -   Japan, with strict gun control, has a homicide rate one-fortieth that of the US
    -   Its rate of non-gun violence is comparable [%b Hemenway2009 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## Historical and Current Interpretations of the Right to Bear Arms

-   Dominant interpretation through most of US history treated the Second Amendment as protecting collective rights
-   Individual right interpretation became dominant only after the 2008 Heller decision
-   Current reading more extreme than prevailing interpretations in most of US history
    -   Chief Justice Warren Burger, a conservative, called the individual rights interpretation "one of the greatest pieces of fraud on the American public" in a 1991 interview [%b Hemenway2009 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## How the NRA Changed Position

-   NRA supported the National Firearms Act of 1934 and the Gun Control Act of 1968
-   After an internal coup in 1977, it adopted an absolutist position opposing any regulation
-   Current absolutist position is a deliberate political project from 1977, not a longstanding tradition
    -   "Revolt at Cincinnati" at the 1977 NRA annual meeting replaced a leadership focused on hunting and marksmanship with one focused on absolutist Second Amendment politics [%b Winkler2011 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## The Racial Pattern of Gun Enforcement

-   Open carry laws have been applied differently based on the race of who is carrying
-   Philando Castile was shot during a traffic stop despite being a licensed concealed-carry holder
-   Pattern of enforcement is documented and consistent
    -   When Black Panthers openly carried rifles at the California State Capitol in 1967, the NRA supported the Mulford Act that banned open carry
    -   One of the most restrictive gun laws in US history at the time [%b Oluo2018 Winkler2011 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## Why the Policy Equilibrium Holds

-   Gun ownership embedded in cultural identity in ways that make policy change politically costly
-   Electoral system structurally overrepresents rural areas where ownership rates are highest
-   Political economy of gun regulation is not primarily about evidence
    -   Senate gives Wyoming (population 580,000) the same number of votes as California (population 39 million)
    -   Rural overrepresentation is the structural basis of gun policy paralysis [%b Achen2017 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## Every Democracy Restricts Some Speech

-   Question is not whether to restrict speech but where to draw the line and who holds the pen
-   Germany prohibits Holocaust denial, France prohibits incitement, and Canada prohibits hate speech
-   US is an outlier in the breadth of its protection, not a global baseline
    -   "Marketplace of ideas" metaphor coined by Justice Holmes in 1919 in a case about anti-war speech
    -   He later dissented from his own doctrine [%b Berlin1991 GartonAsh2016 Popper2011 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## What the Harm Principle Misses

-   Mill's harm principle considers direct harm to identifiable individuals
-   Does not weigh diffuse, cumulative, and unequally distributed harms across groups
-   Dehumanizing speech harms communities rather than specific identifiable individuals
    -   Jeremy Waldron's 2012 book *The Harm in Hate Speech* argued that hate speech undermines the standing of target groups as equal members of society
    -   A harm Mill's framework cannot capture [%b Waldron2012 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## Dehumanizing Speech and Real-World Violence

-   Radio Mille Collines broadcast Tutsi dehumanization before and during the Rwandan genocide
-   Connection between speech and subsequent violence is documented in multiple cases
-   Question is whether the connection is close enough to justify restriction
    -   *Machete Season*, Jacques Semelin's oral history of the genocide, documents how radio broadcasts were used to direct killers to specific locations in real time [%b Dallaire2005 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## Who Gets Restricted in Practice

-   Speech restriction tools have historically been used against political dissidents and labor organizers
-   US Espionage Act (1917) was used against anti-war speech, not German spies
-   History shapes who trusts restriction and who does not
    -   Eugene Debs received a 10-year sentence under the Espionage Act for opposing the draft in 1918 and ran for president from prison, receiving nearly a million votes [%b GartonAsh2016 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## The Moral Panic Pattern

-   A coalition of moral entrepreneurs identifies a new medium as uniquely dangerous to youth
-   Politicians respond with legislative proposals
-   Researchers produce studies that support restriction
-   Coalition forms around restriction regardless of evidence because restriction serves multiple interests
    -   Fredric Wertham's 1954 book *Seduction of the Innocent* blamed comic books for juvenile delinquency
    -   Subsequent researchers found his data fabricated [%b Markey2017 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## What the Media Violence Research Actually Shows

-   Empirical literature on media violence is large, contentious, and has not survived the replication crisis
-   Laboratory measures of "aggression" bear uncertain relationship to real-world violence
-   Countries with the highest violent video game consumption do not show correspondingly higher violence rates
    -   Christopher Ferguson's meta-analysis found that publication bias explained most of the apparent relationship between media violence exposure and aggression [%b Ferguson2015 Grossman1995 Grossman1999 Markey2017 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## Cross-National Comparisons Undermine Simple Causal Claims

-   Japan consumes violent media at high rates and has very low violence rates
-   US consumes violent media at high rates and has high gun violence rates
-   Difference is not the media
-   It is the guns
    -   Correlation between video game spending and gun deaths across countries is actually negative: countries that spend more on games have fewer gun deaths [%b Markey2017 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## How Algorithms Encode Past Discrimination

-   COMPAS predicts recidivism risk using criminal history, age, employment, and residential stability
-   These inputs are legal
-   Also deeply correlated with race as a result of differential policing
-   Algorithm does not need to see race
-   The other inputs serve as proxies
    -   ProPublica's 2016 analysis found that COMPAS falsely flagged Black defendants as future criminals at twice the rate it falsely flagged white defendants [%b Chouldechova2017 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## The Fairness Impossibility

-   No algorithm can simultaneously achieve equal false positive rates across groups and calibration
    when base rates differ between groups
-   Mathematical consequence, not an engineering problem
-   ProPublica and Northpointe were both right
-   They were measuring different things
    -   Alexandra Chouldechova proved mathematically in 2017 that the three fairness criteria cannot all be satisfied simultaneously when base rates differ between groups [%b Chouldechova2017 %]

</section>
<section class="slide" markdown="1">

## A Decision You Cannot Inspect or Contest

-   Proprietary risk assessment tools are trade secrets
-   Defendants cannot examine the algorithm used to score them
-   Automation creates the appearance of objectivity while encoding the same biases as human judgment
-   A person's liberty can be determined by a calculation they are legally prevented from examining
    -   In *State v. Loomis* (2016), the Wisconsin Supreme Court held that using a proprietary algorithm in sentencing did not violate due process
    -   The defendant had no right to inspect the formula [%b Eubanks2018 Khera2019 Oneil2016 Pasquale2015 %]

</section>
