## Who is this book for?

Every lesson should aim to meet the needs of specific people [Wils2019](BIB).
These [learner personas][learner-persona] define the intended audience for ours:

-   Edon is an undergraduate in computer science.
    He enjoys learning about algorithms and data structures,
    but finds the discussion of intersectionality and decolonization in his literature course
    boring, bewildering, and condescending in equal measure.
    However,
    he is increasingly uncomfortable comparing
    the three jobs his mother has worked since arriving in Canada from the Balkans in the 1990s
    and the "move fast and break things" bravado of many of his fellow students.
    These lessons will give him better tools for analyzing the world
    than Silicon Valley's libertarianism-lite.

-   Tambara is leads a four-person team at a website development company.
    As the only woman of color in management in her company,
    she has been tasked with "doing something about diversity" by its founders
    (who are all white or Asian men).
    She has learned the hard way that workshops on unconscious bias and inclusivity
    have little or no impact on her colleagues' behavior,
    so these lessons will give her material that will hold her colleagues' attention
    long enough to actually change some of their minds.
